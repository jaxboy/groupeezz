Swedish House Mafia (minus Steve Angello)

Well, it’s been circulating for about a two weeks now that former Swedish House Mafia members Sebastian Ingrosso and Axwell have formed a new production duo known as Departures, and it seems like a lot of EDM fans are up in arms saying that their “One Last Tour” wasn’t a last anything at all.
True, if you’re going to see a Departures performance in the future, you’ll probably be getting a similar show just without the third “Mafia” member, Steve Angello, but my speculation is that these two couldn’t keep the Swedish House Mafia name for legal reasons.
For whatever reason Ingrosso and Axwell decided to leave, it must’ve had something to do with how they couldn’t work with Steve Angello anymore. I mean c’mon, you’re choosing a name called Departures, as in, we’re departing from the one person we had differences with?
It’s cool, Steve Angello is rich, has his own label (Size Records), and has a beard. Take THAT Ingrosso and Axwell.

MAR

18

Kaskade

As I sit here finishing up Kaskade’s live set from this past weekend at Ultra Music Festival in Miami, I can’t help but amazed about how this guy goes out on stage and just destroys every show he plays at. His blend of vocals from his signature tracks combined with current Electro/Progressive House bangers is the perfect formula for a DJ set that never sounds the same. Sure, he’s using the same acapella but when he combines them with different buildups and drops, it’s always creating a new experience for the listener.
I’m not a self-proclaimed house guru, in fact I never really listened to house much when I first started DJing 13 years ago, but I do remember hearing Kaskade’s name being brought up by fellow DJs that were also underground ravers and house heads. Fast forward to now, when EDM is at the height of it’s popularity in the U.S., and you have Kaskade who is on another level in terms of DJing, producing, and super-stardom (is that a word?)
I guess to sum it all up, at the end of the day I give Kaskade a tremendous about of respect not only for the music that he provides to the masses, but for him being a lasting figure in the house music scene, even when there weren’t millions of people selling out public festivals across the country. As a DJ, I give him a ton of credit for riding out the days when he wasn’t a marque name and sticking to his passion and doing it for the love of music and the DJ craft, because as most DJs might know, this industry isn’t always bright lights and raised hands.
Cheers to you Kaskade, keep representing the music and the DJ!
